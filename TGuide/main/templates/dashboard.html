Best Tourist Spots in Kerala

Kochi

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant port city that blends colonial history with modern charm. It is famous for its Chinese fishing nets, Jew Town, Mattancherry Palace, and Fort Kochi.

  • Best Time to visit: October to March
  • Weather: Warm (17°C to 33°C), monsoon brings heavy rain.
  • Top Attractions: Fort Kochi, Chinese Fishing Nets, Mattancherry Palace, Kerala Folklore Museum, Marine Drive, etc.
  • Activities:
    • Take a heritage walk in Fort Kochi.
    • Enjoy a sunset cruise in the backwaters.
    • Try local seafood delicacies at waterfront restaurants.

Munnar

Munnar is a breathtaking hill station renowned for its expansive tea plantations and misty landscapes in the Western Ghats.

  • Best Time to visit: September to March
  • Weather: Cool (15°C to 25°C), with lush greenery during monsoons.
  • Top Attractions: Tea Gardens, Eravikulam National Park, Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, Anamudi Peak.
  • Activities:
    • Stroll through the rolling tea estates and visit the Tea Museum.
    • Trek to nearby hills for panoramic vistas.
    • Boating at Mattupetty Dam.

Marari Beach

Marari Beach is a serene coastal retreat ideal for unwinding on pristine sands, away from the usual hustle.

  • Best Time to visit: November to February
  • Weather: Warm and sunny (22°C to 30°C); summers are humid, while monsoons provide a soothing rain.
  • Top Attractions: Marari Beach, nearby fishing villages, local Ayurvedic spas.
  • Activities:
    • Relax on the quiet and unspoiled shoreline.
    • Experience authentic Ayurvedic treatments.
    • Cycle through the charming coastal villages.

Alappuzha

Also known as Alleppey, Alappuzha is famous for its enchanting backwaters, elegant houseboat cruises, and a laid-back coastal vibe.

  • Best Time to visit: October to February
  • Weather: Warm (up to 33°C); summers can be hot while monsoons bring heavy rains, enhancing its lush landscapes.
  • Top Attractions: Backwater cruises, Alappuzha Beach, historic lighthouse, temples, and markets.
  • Activities:
    • Enjoy an overnight houseboat experience in the backwaters.
    • Witness the vibrant Nehru Trophy Boat Race.
    • Explore the local culture and heritage sites.

Wayanad

Wayanad is a nature lover’s paradise nestled in the Western Ghats, famous for its verdant landscapes, ancient caves, waterfalls, and rich wildlife.

  • Best Time to visit: October to May
  • Weather: Pleasant (20°C to 36°C); monsoons intensify its lush beauty.
  • Top Attractions: Edakkal Caves, Banasura Sagar Dam, Chembra Peak, Soochipara Falls, and the Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Activities:
    • Trek to Chembra Peak and see the iconic heart-shaped lake.
    • Explore prehistoric carvings in Edakkal Caves.
    • Embark on a wildlife safari in the sanctuary.

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam Beach is celebrated for its crescent-shaped shores and vibrant atmosphere, offering both relaxation and an array of water sports.

  • Best Time to visit: September to March
  • Weather: Warm (up to 37°C) with mild winters and monsoons that bring in lively waves.
  • Top Attractions: Lighthouse Beach, Hawa Beach, marine aquarium, and Ayurvedic spas.
  • Activities:
    • Enjoy water sports such as surfing, parasailing, and snorkeling.
    • Take a leisurely walk along the picturesque promenade.
    • Indulge in rejuvenating Ayurvedic therapies by the sea.
